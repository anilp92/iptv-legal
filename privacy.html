IPTV Watcher — Privacy Policy

Effective 5 July 2026

IPTV Watcher is a private, limited-audience app: a client for a self-hosted IPTV server that you or your household operate. It is not a public streaming service and provides no content of its own.

The short version

Everything the app sends goes to one place only: the server you configure at setup — a server owned and run by your own household. There are no third-party analytics, no advertising, no tracking, and no sale or sharing of data with anyone.

Data the app processes

  • Account data — your sign-in name and profile selection, sent to your server to authenticate you.
  • Viewing state — watch progress, favourites and preferences, stored on your server so playback resumes across your household's devices.
  • Device information — a device name and identifier, registered with your server for device management and casting between your own devices.
  • Playback diagnostics — quality-of-experience measurements (buffering, bitrate changes, start-up time) and crash reports, sent to your server to help its operator keep playback working well. You can switch diagnostics off at any time in Settings → Privacy inside the app.

Where data goes

All of the above is transmitted over TLS exclusively to the server address configured in the app. The operator of that server (a member of your household) controls the data on it. Diagnostic events are typically retained for 90 days (aggregates up to 365 days) before automatic deletion. The app contacts no other service, with one exception: push notifications are delivered through the Apple Push Notification service, as on any iOS app; their content originates from your own server.

Local network access

On first run the app asks for local-network permission solely to discover your server on your home network (Bonjour). Declining it only disables automatic discovery — you can type the server address instead.

Children

Household child profiles are protected by the server's parental controls. No data beyond the categories above is collected for any profile.

Your choices

  • Turn diagnostics off: Settings → Privacy in the app.
  • Access, correction or deletion of household data: contact your server's operator — for this app's audience, that is a member of your own household.
